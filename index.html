About Me

I am a PhD student at Purdue University specializing in computational hydrology and artificial intelligence applications in water science. My research focuses on developing intelligent, scalable tools that bridge hydrological process modeling, machine learning, and geospatial data engineering for scientific research and decision-making.

With a strong foundation in Civil Engineering from Aligarh Muslim University and Earth Sciences from IIT Guwahati, I bring interdisciplinary expertise to address complex water resource challenges. I am particularly interested in automating hydrological workflows, integrating open datasets, and developing reproducible modeling frameworks through APIs and computational tools.

I am passionate about open science and democratizing access to hydrological modeling tools. My goal is to contribute to sustainable water management through innovative research that combines traditional hydrological understanding with modern computational approaches.

Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of hydrology, machine learning, and geospatial analysis. I am particularly interested in developing computational frameworks that enhance our understanding of hydrological processes through data-driven approaches and reproducible scientific software.

Current research areas include GRACE data downscaling for improved terrestrial water storage estimation, stochastic rainfall modeling, catchment response analysis, and the integration of remote sensing data with traditional hydrological models. I am also interested in developing user-friendly platforms and APIs that make complex hydrological analyses more accessible to the research community.
